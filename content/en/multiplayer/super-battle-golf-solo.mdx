<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Core Mechanics and Controls">

Success in **super battle golf solo** depends on your ability to manipulate the ball while managing your character's movement. Unlike traditional golf games, you are physically running to your ball after every shot. This adds a layer of "locomotion strategy" where you must decide whether to sprint toward your ball or detour to pick up an item box.

The power meter in Super Battle Golf moves significantly faster than in most golf simulators. Timing is everything. If you overcharge a shot, the ball will slice aggressively. While this is usually a penalty, skilled solo players can use a "locked-on" overcharge shot to beam an opponent in the head, granting a massive speed boost and disrupting their momentum.

| Action | Control/Input | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Adjust Angle** | Mouse Wheel / Keys 1-4 | Use hotkeys for instant 0° or 45° shifts |
| **Charge Shot** | Hold Right Click | Release at 90% to avoid the overcharge slice |
| **Lock-On** | Hold Right Click (near ball) | Essential for hitting moving targets/players |
| **Sprint** | Shift (Standard) | Only use when your stamina/coffee is active |
| **Intercept** | Character Body | Run into an opponent's ball to stop its momentum |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Overcharge Risk">
Overcharging your shot causes a severe curve. Unless you are specifically trying to hit another player with a "grudge shot," always aim for the "sweet spot" on the meter. A missed long-distance chip can cost you 10-15 seconds in travel time.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Biome Navigation and Winning Strategies">

There are 27 holes distributed across three biomes. Each biome introduces unique environmental hazards that a **super battle golf solo** player must master. The terrain readout at the bottom of the screen is your best friend—it shows the elevation and surface type along your current aim line.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Initial Drive">
    Use the "Mario Kart style" boost at the start. Time your swing perfectly as the countdown hits zero to get an early lead on the first item boxes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mid-Course Navigation">
    Focus on "Par Time" rather than just speed. If you fall out of bounds, you reset to the last checkpoint. It is often faster to take a safe shot than to risk a "hero shot" over a water hazard.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Final Green Battle">
    Once the first person sinks their ball, a 20-30 second "Death Timer" begins. If you aren't on the green by then, you must panic-putt. Don't be afraid to physically stand in front of an opponent's ball to intercept it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Biome Type | Key Hazards | Recommended Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tropical** | Water hazards, sand traps | Focus on high-arc chips to clear palms |
| **Desert** | Moving dunes, narrow canyons | Use low-angle shots to avoid wind-like drift |
| **Urban/Industrial** | Moving platforms, verticality | Prioritize Jump Boots for vertical shortcuts |

<Callout type="info" title="Scoring Arcana">
The scoring system is complex, factoring in speed, strokes, and combat bonuses. However, in most public lobbies, the primary goal is simply to finish before the timer expires. Zero points are awarded for failing to hole out.
</Callout>
