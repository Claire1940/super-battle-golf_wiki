When managing your shots on the green, putting and chipping require opposite approaches to distance control. If you are putting, you generally want to putt over the target to ensure the ball has enough momentum to reach the cup. Conversely, when chipping, you want to chip under the target to account for the natural roll and bounce of the ball upon landing.

| Swing Type | Execution Strategy | Target Alignment | Roll Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Putting | Putt over the target | Beyond the cup | Low bounce, direct roll |
| Chipping | Chip under the target | Short of the cup | High arc, significant roll |
| Homing Shot | Maximize energy bar | Lock-on target | Direct path to objective |

Additionally, keeping an eye on your energy bar is crucial. Once you max out your energy, you gain the ability to execute a homing shot. This special shot allows you to lock onto specific targets, including the hole or even deployed mines, ensuring your ball lands exactly where you need it to go.

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Alert">
Be careful when executing homing shots on mines. If you drop a mine directly in front of you to lock onto it, you will consume your entire stack of mines instantly.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Weaponry and Hazard Management">

Combat is just as important as your golf swing in this game. Players have access to a wide array of weapons and tools, including guns, explosives, golf carts, shields, and orbital lasers. Knowing how to deploy these items and how to handle hazards left by other players is essential for maintaining your lead.

Mines are a common obstacle on the course. If you find an armed mine blocking your path, you do not have to avoid it entirely. You can disarm it by pressing the Alt key to slow walk, and then pressing E to pick it up. This adds the mine to your inventory, turning a hazardous obstacle into a useful defensive tool.

| Item Type | Primary Function | Counter Strategy | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Armed Mine | Area denial and explosive damage | Slow walk with Alt and press E to pick up | Medium |
| Shield | Blocks incoming projectiles and melee | Use high-power club swings to bypass | Low |
| Orbital Laser | Large area-of-effect strike | Move out of the red targeting reticle | High |
| Golf Cart | Rapid map traversal and ramming | Use explosives or high-power swings | Medium |

If you decide to throw a mine instead of dropping it, a timer will begin. You must reach the mine and interact with it before it arms, or it will backfire and detonate on you. Managing these timers while dodging opponent attacks requires practice and situational awareness.

<Section icon={Map} title="Melee Combat and Speed Optimization">

Engaging in melee combat is not just a defensive measure; it is a core component of your movement strategy. Hitting opponents with your club grants you a temporary speed boost, allowing you to run faster across the fairway. Therefore, you should actively look for opportunities to strike nearby players as you move toward the hole.

When fighting other players, utilize a two-step approach to control the field. A simple, no-power club hit is sufficient to knock an opponent off balance and send them to the ground. Once they are down, you can charge up a max-power hit to launch them far away from their ball or even off the map entirely.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Contact">
    Approach an opponent and perform a quick, no-power swing with your club to knock them down.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Charge the Shot">
    While the opponent is recovering on the ground, begin charging a max-power swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Launch the Target">
    Release the max-power swing to send the opponent off the map, clearing your path to the green.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Using this combat sequence effectively keeps your opponents disoriented and gives you the space needed to plan your next shot. Remember to balance combat with progression, as spending too much time fighting can allow other players to slip ahead to the hole.

<Callout type="success" title="Speed Advantage">
Always prioritize landing at least one quick hit on an opponent during transitions between shots to maintain your movement speed buff.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Endgame Scoring and Item Farming">

The scoring system rewards speed and efficiency. Points are awarded based on who reaches the hole first and who completes the course in the fewest swings. However, once three players have successfully finished the hole, no extra placement points are awarded for subsequent finishes.

If you find yourself trailing behind and realize that three players have already completed the course, you should shift your strategy. Instead of rushing to finish the hole for minimal points, use your remaining time to gather items and resources. This prepares you for the next round and denies items to your opponents.

| Match Position | Primary Objective | Scoring Potential | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st to 3rd Place | Finish the hole quickly | High placement points | Focus on fast, clean shots |
| 4th Place or Lower | Gather items and resource boxes | Low placement points | Focus on inventory building |

A highly effective strategy for item gathering is to align your character on an item box before taking your final shot into the hole. While your character is frozen during the post-round transition, they will continue to pick up items automatically until their inventory is completely full.
