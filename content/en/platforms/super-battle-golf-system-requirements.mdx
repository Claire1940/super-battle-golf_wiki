<Section icon={Globe} title="Network Requirements and Multiplayer Stability">

Since Super Battle Golf is a real-time multiplayer game, your network stability is just as critical as your hardware specifications. The game uses a client-server architecture to synchronize up to eight players, their golf balls, and various combat items like the Thunderstorm or Orbital Laser.

To ensure you don't disconnect during a crucial final putt, follow these network guidelines:

- **Ping**: Aim for a ping under 80ms to the nearest regional server.
- **Connection Type**: An Ethernet (wired) connection is significantly more stable than 2.4GHz Wi-Fi.
- **Bandwidth**: While the game doesn't use much data, background downloads (like Steam updates) should be paused.
- **Firewall**: Ensure the game executable is allowed through your Windows Firewall to prevent lobby joining errors.

<Callout type="info" title="Lobby Connection Info">
Most "Failed to Join" errors are caused by mismatched game versions. Always ensure your Steam client has downloaded the latest 2026 patches before attempting to join a private friend lobby.
</Callout>

If you are hosting a private lobby for friends, the host's upload speed becomes more relevant. A minimum upload speed of 5 Mbps is recommended for a smooth 8-player experience if you are the designated host.
