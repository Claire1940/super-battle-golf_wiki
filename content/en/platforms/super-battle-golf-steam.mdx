<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Swing: Physics & Controls">

Navigating the courses in this game requires a solid grasp of its arcade physics and control layout. Unlike traditional golf games with complex wind calculations and club selections, this title simplifies the formula to keep the action fast. You control your character in a third-person perspective, running up to your ball and lining up shots using a dynamic power meter. Precision is key, as mistiming your swing can send your ball flying into out-of-bounds hazards, forcing a costly respawn at the last unlocked checkpoint.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Positioning and Aiming">
    Run to your stationary ball and hold down the right mouse button to lock your camera perspective. Use your mouse wheel or keyboard hotkeys to adjust your launch angle from 0 to 45 degrees.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Timing the Power Meter">
    Hold your swing button to charge the power bar. Release it exactly at the peak for maximum distance. Be careful not to overcharge, as this causes the ball to slice wildly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sprinting and Locomotion">
    Once the ball is in flight, immediately sprint after it. Use your jump ability to navigate platforms, shortcuts, and hazards while keeping an eye on surrounding opponents.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Putting on the Green">
    When you reach the green, drop your angle to 0 degrees. Use gentle taps of the power meter to slide the ball smoothly into the cup before the death timer expires.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Understanding how shot angles affect your ball's trajectory is crucial for shortcuts. While a 45-degree angle is ideal for clearing massive chasms, lower angles are perfect for keeping the ball low under obstacles or rolling along flat fairways.

| Shot Angle | Primary Use Case | Maximum Range | Accuracy Level |
| 45 Degrees | Clearing large chasms, long-distance chips | Excellent | Moderate |
| 30 Degrees | Mid-range fairway progression, bypassing low arches | Good | High |
| 15 Degrees | Short-range approaches, navigating tight tunnels | Moderate | Very High |
| 0 Degrees | Putting on the green, rolling past close-range hazards | Low | Pinpoint |

<Callout type="warning" title="Overcharge Penalty">
Overcharging your swing meter past the maximum threshold does not grant extra distance. Instead, it introduces a severe curve or slice to your trajectory, often sending your ball directly into water hazards or off the map. Keep your power levels controlled.
</Callout>
