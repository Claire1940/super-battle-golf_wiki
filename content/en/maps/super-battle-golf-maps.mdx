<Section icon={Shield} title="Strategic Checkpoint Management">

Checkpoints are your lifeline on the more treacherous **super battle golf maps**. Skipping a checkpoint might seem like a time-saver, but in a game where seven other players are actively trying to knock you out, it is often a fatal mistake. Securing a checkpoint ensures that even if you are hit by a Railgun or fall into the ocean, you only lose a few seconds of progress rather than the entire hole.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Assessment">
    Identify the location of the next checkpoint immediately after your opening drive. Look for the glowing gate or platform.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Safe Approach">
    Approach the checkpoint from a side angle. Most players aim directly for the center, creating a "kill zone" for area-of-effect items like **Thunderstorm**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Activation and Exit">
    Once the checkpoint is triggered, do not stand still. Move toward the next playable area immediately to avoid being "spawn camped" by trailing players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Shortcut Decision">
    Only attempt a major route skip if you have already secured the nearest checkpoint. This provides a safety net if the shortcut fails.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Recovery Tactic">
If you are knocked out, use the brief invulnerability window after respawning to move through dangerous hazards or contested areas that were previously blocked.
</Callout>

</Section>
